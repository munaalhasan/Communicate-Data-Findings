Investigation Overview¶

  1. Trip Count and User Base:
  • Subscribers significantly outnumber customers in trip count across all days, underscoring their reliance on the service for frequent, routine travel, such as commuting.
  • Despite lower trip counts, customers spend more time on trips compared to subscribers, hinting at less routine and more recreational trip purposes.
  1. Trip Duration and Patterns:
  • Customers consistently exhibit higher average trip durations compared to subscribers, regardless of the day.
  • Customer durations peak on weekends (Saturday and Sunday), suggesting usage is largely driven by leisure activities.
  • Subscribers maintain stable trip durations throughout the week, reflecting routine and predictable usage.
  1. Purpose of Trips:
  • Subscriber trips are characterized by frequent, shorter journeys focusing on efficiency and routine needs.
  • Customer trips are fewer but longer, with a clear focus on leisure or occasional use, such as recreational outings or tourism.
  1. Distance and Weekend Trends:
  • Customer trips are notably longer in both duration and distance compared to those of subscribers across all days of the week.
  • Both groups show longer durations for shorter distances on weekends compared to weekdays, reinforcing the idea that weekends are primarily for leisure use.

Dataset Overview and Executive Summary¶

This dataset contains information about bike-sharing system rides, specifically from February 2019. It includes details about users, such as age and gender, along with trip data such as the start and end points, stations, and trip durations. Additionally, it captures information on the type of user (customer or Subscriber).

def user_type_plot(bi_var=None,multi_var=None):
    plt.figure(figsize=(10,5))
    user_type_color={'Customer': sns.color_palette('dark:#5A9_r')[0], 'Subscriber':sns.color_palette('dark:#5A9_r')[2]}
    if bi_var:
        sns.countplot(data=df, x='start_weekday', hue='user_type', order=ordered_days, palette=user_type_color) 
        title='Distribution'
    elif multi_var:
        sns.barplot(data = df, x = 'start_weekday', y = 'duration_minutes', hue = 'user_type', order=ordered_days, palette=user_type_color)
        plt.ylabel('Duration (minutes)')
        title='Trip Duration'

    plt.title(f'{title} of Customers and Subscribers Throughout the Week')    
    plt.legend(loc = 0, ncol = 3, framealpha = 1, title = 'User Type')
    plt.xlabel('')

Conclusion¶

The analysis highlights distinct usage behaviors between customers and subscribers. Subscribers prioritize volume and efficiency with short, frequent trips, often for commuting. In contrast, customers focus on leisure with fewer but longer trips, primarily during weekends.
